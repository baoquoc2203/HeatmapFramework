What's different about ADA vs. the BTC version

One real cycle, not several structurally simpler

ADA only has one true full Wyckoff-scale cycle in its history: the multi-year 2018–2020 base, the 2021 markup to $3.09, the 2021 distribution top, and the markdown that's continued — with interruptions — ever since. Unlike BTC's repeating accumulation/markup/distribution/markdown wheel, ADA hasn't built a second comparable base; the 2023 and late-2024 rallies were partial recoveries inside a longer downtrend, not full reaccumulation cycles by the same standard applied to BTC.

Fear & Greed here is borrowed, not ADA-specific context, not a clean signal

This matters more for ADA than it did for BTC: the Fear & Greed Index is built from BTC-dominant market data. It tells you about crypto-wide mood, which correlates with ADA but isn't a direct read on ADA's own market structure. Treat this row as macro backdrop, not as confirmation of an ADA-specific bottom or top the way it worked for BTC.

Mercury & Shmita same verdict as BTC

No different from the BTC chart: Mercury windows scatter across every phase with no pattern. Shmita is still n=1 — and while it's a notable coincidence that ADA's all-time high fell inside the one Shmita year in this window, a single occurrence on a single asset is exactly the kind of pattern that looks meaningful and isn't.

Bottom line: ADA's chart has less real structure to work with than BTC's — fewer cycles, no asset-specific sentiment index, and a much steeper, more singular boom-bust shape. The framework's own live tracking (right of the cyan seam) remains the only segment that's a genuine real-time record, exactly as with BTC.
